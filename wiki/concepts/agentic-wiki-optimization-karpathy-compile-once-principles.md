# Agentic Wiki Optimization per Karpathy Compile-Once Principles

## Overview

Agentic wiki optimization applies Karpathy's compile-once principles to knowledge management, emphasizing durable answers, file-back behavior, and advanced linting for implicit concepts and cross-references. This approach aims to make agent workflows more robust, maintainable, and context-aware.

## How It Works

Karpathy's compile-once principle advocates for knowledge artifacts that are compiled and kept current, rather than being re-derived on every query. In the context of agentic wikis, this means that answers generated by agents should be filed back as persistent wiki pages, ensuring that future queries benefit from prior work.

Agent workflows are upgraded to include advanced linting, which detects implicit concepts (ideas not yet codified as pages), missing cross-references, graph orphans (nodes with no links), and duplicate source hashes. Prompts for agents such as wiki-curator, wiki-lint, and wiki-query are enhanced to be graph-aware, enabling them to propose new links, suggest merges, and identify structural defects.

Log prefix enforcement is introduced, requiring all log entries to follow a standardized format (e.g., '## [YYYY-MM-DD] op | Title'), which enables easy auditing and retrieval of recent changes. Community detection and god-node analysis are used to identify hubs and structural distortions in the knowledge graph, such as publishers acting as topical hubs.

Technical implementation involves updating agent prompt files, expanding lint checks, and integrating graph statistics from APIs. Deduplication logic is improved to include fuzzy matching, and publisher entities are down-weighted in graph builder scripts to reduce false adjacencies. The system is validated by rebuilding the graph and verifying improved community clustering.

## Key Properties

- **Compile-Once Knowledge Artifacts:** Answers are filed back as durable wiki pages, not re-derived per query.
- **Graph-Aware Agent Prompts:** Agents propose links, merges, and identify structural defects based on graph statistics.
- **Advanced Linting:** Detects implicit concepts, missing cross-references, graph orphans, and source-hash duplicates.
- **Log Prefix Enforcement:** Standardizes log entries for auditability and retrieval.

## Limitations

Compile-once artifacts require ongoing maintenance to stay current. Advanced linting can be computationally intensive and may generate false positives. Down-weighting publisher hubs may inadvertently reduce legitimate connections. Agent prompt upgrades require careful tuning to avoid overwhelming users with suggestions.
