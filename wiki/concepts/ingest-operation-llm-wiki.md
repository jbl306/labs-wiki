# Ingest Operation in LLM Wiki

## Overview

The ingest operation involves adding new sources to the LLM Wiki, where the LLM processes the content and updates the wiki accordingly.

## How It Works

When a new source is added, the LLM reads the document, extracts key information, and creates or updates relevant pages in the wiki. This process may include summarizing the source, updating the index, and maintaining cross-references. Users can choose to supervise the process or automate it entirely.

## Key Properties

- **Source Integration:** New sources are integrated into the wiki with summaries and updates to relevant pages.
- **User Involvement:** Users can guide the LLM during the ingest process to emphasize specific aspects of the source.
- **Batch or Single Ingest:** Sources can be ingested one at a time or in batches, depending on user preference.

## Relationship to Other Concepts

- **[[LLM Wiki]]** — Ingest is a core operation in the LLM Wiki system.

## Practical Applications

Used to add new knowledge to the wiki and ensure it remains up-to-date.
