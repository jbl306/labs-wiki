# Quality Evaluation of Auto-Ingested Wiki Content

## Overview

Quality evaluation assesses the factual accuracy, completeness, and structural integrity of wiki pages generated by the auto-ingest pipeline from raw sources. This process ensures that the knowledge base reliably reflects source materials and maintains high standards for downstream use by AI agents and human readers.

## How It Works

The evaluation process involves several steps:

1. **Source Identification**: Identify raw sources ingested on a given date (e.g., April 10, 2026), including arXiv papers and GitHub repositories.

2. **Ground Truth Fetching**: Retrieve abstracts or official documentation from source URLs to serve as reference.

3. **Wiki Page Collection**: Gather all wiki pages generated from these sources, including concept, entity, and synthesis pages.

4. **Parallel Reading Agents**: Launch background AI agents tasked with reading and analyzing the generated wiki pages in parallel, each focusing on a specific source.

5. **Evaluation Dimensions**:
   - *Factual Accuracy*: Verify that claims in wiki pages match source content.
   - *Completeness*: Check coverage of key concepts and entities.
   - *Cross-Reference Quality*: Assess the validity and relevance of wikilinks.
   - *Frontmatter Compliance*: Ensure metadata fields conform to schema requirements.
   - *Depth*: Evaluate whether content is substantive or superficial.

6. **Logging and Auditing**: Use logs to confirm ingestion success and identify any missing or silent failures.

7. **Synthesis**: Aggregate findings to produce an overall quality score and identify systematic issues for remediation.

This evaluation is critical for maintaining trustworthiness and utility of the wiki as a knowledge resource.

## Key Properties

- **Multi-Dimensional Assessment:** Combines factual, structural, and metadata checks for comprehensive quality analysis.
- **Parallel Agent Readers:** Uses AI agents to scale evaluation across multiple sources and pages concurrently.
- **Ground Truth Comparison:** Directly compares wiki content against original source abstracts and documentation.
- **Audit Logging:** Tracks ingestion operations to detect missing or incomplete processing.

## Limitations

Evaluation is limited by the availability and completeness of source materials. Some ingests may fail silently or lack log entries, complicating assessment. Automated agents may not fully capture nuanced errors or omissions without human review. Scoring and promotion mechanisms are still under development.

## Example

For the April 10 ingest batch, four background agents were launched: eval-lottery-ticket, eval-llm-reasoning, eval-axi-repo, and eval-autoskills. Each agent read 6-7 wiki pages generated from respective sources, checking claims against arXiv abstracts or GitHub READMEs. The evaluation noted a missing log entry for one source, indicating a potential silent failure. Quality scores were all zero, pending further scoring implementation.
